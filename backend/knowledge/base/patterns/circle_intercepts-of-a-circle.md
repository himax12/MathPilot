### Example 1 (Year: 2010)

**Question:**

The circle $${x^2} + {y^2} = 4x + 8y + 5$$ intersects the line $$3x - 4y = m$$ at two distinct points if :

**Solution:**

Circle $${x^2} + {y^2} - 4x - 8y - 5 = 0$$ 
<br><br>Center $$=(2,4),$$ Radius $$ = \sqrt {4 + 16 + 5}  = 5$$
<br><br>If circle is intersecting line $$3x-4y=m,$$ at two distinct points. 
<br><br>$$ \Rightarrow $$ length of perpendicular from center to the line $$ &lt; $$ radius 
<br><br>$$ \Rightarrow {{\left| {6 - 16 - m} \right|} \over 5} &lt; 5 \Rightarrow \left| {10 + m} \right| &lt; 25$$
<br><br>$$ \Rightarrow  - 25 &lt; m + 10 &lt; 25 \Rightarrow  - 35 &lt; m &lt; 15$$

### Example 2 (Year: 2018)

**Question:**

If a circle C, whose radius is 3, touches externally the circle, 
<br>$${x^2} + {y^2} + 2x - 4y - 4 = 0$$ at the point (2, 2), then the length of the intercept cut by this circle C, on the x-axis is equal to : 

**Solution:**

Given circle is : 
<br><br>x<sup>2</sup> + y<sup>2</sup> + 2x $$-$$ 4y $$-$$4 = 0
<br><br>$$\therefore\,\,\,$$ its center is ($$-$$ 1, 2) and radius is 3 units.
<br><br>Let A = (x, y) be the center of the circle C
<br><br>$$ \therefore $$$$\,\,\,$$ $${{x - 1} \over 2}$$ = 2 $$ \Rightarrow $$ x = 5 and $${{y + 2} \over 2}$$ = 2 $$ \Rightarrow $$ y = 2
<br><br>So the center of C is  (5, 2)  and its radius is 3
<br><br>$$\therefore\,\,\,$$ Equation of center C is : 
<br><br>x<sup>2</sup> + y<sup>2</sup> $$-$$ 10x $$-$$ 4y + 20 = 0
<br><br>$$\therefore\,\,\,$$ The length of the intercept it cuts on the x-axis 
<br><br>= 2$$\sqrt {{g^2} - c}  = 2\sqrt {25 - 20}  = 2\sqrt 5 $$
