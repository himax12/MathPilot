### Example 1 (Year: 2005)

**Question:**

If in a $$\Delta ABC$$, the altitudes from the vertices $$A, B, C$$ on opposite sides are in H.P, then $$\sin A,\sin B,\sin C$$ are in :

**Solution:**

$$\Delta  = {1 \over 2}{p_1}a = {1 \over 2}{p_2}b = {1 \over 2}{p_3}b$$
<br><br>$${p_1},{p_2},{p_3},$$ are in $$H.P.$$ 
<br><br>$$ \Rightarrow {{2\Delta } \over a},{{2\Delta } \over b},{{2\Delta } \over c}$$ are in $$H.P.$$ 
<br><br>$$ \Rightarrow {1 \over a},{1 \over b},{1 \over c},$$ are in $$H.P.$$
<br><br>$$ \Rightarrow a,b,c$$ are in $$A.P.$$
<br><br>$$ \Rightarrow $$ $$K\sin A,K\sin B,K\sin C$$ are in $$A.P.$$
<br><br>$$ \Rightarrow $$ $$\sin A,\sin B,\sin C$$ are in $$A.P.$$
