### Example 1 (Year: 2002)

**Question:**

The sides of a triangle are $$3x + 4y,$$ $$4x + 3y$$ and $$5x + 5y$$ where $$x$$, $$y&gt;0$$ then the triangle is :

**Solution:**

Let $$\,\,\,\,a = 3x + 4y,b = 4x + 3y$$ 
<br><br>and $$c = 5x + 5y$$ 
<br><br>as $$\,\,\,\,x,y &gt; 0,c = 5x + 5y$$ is the largest side
<br><br>$$\therefore$$ $$C$$ is the largest angle. Now
<br><br>$$\cos \,C = {{{{\left( {3x + 4y} \right)}^2} + {{\left( {4x + 3y} \right)}^3} - {{\left( {5x + 5y} \right)}^2}} \over {2\left( {3x + 4y} \right)\left( {4x + 3y} \right)}}$$
<br><br>$$ = {{ - 2xy} \over {2\left( {3x + 4y} \right)\left( {4x + 3y} \right)}} &lt; 0$$
<br><br>$$\therefore$$ $$C$$ is obtuse angle $$ \Rightarrow \Delta ABC$$ is obtuse angled

### Example 2 (Year: 2004)

**Question:**

The sides of a triangle are $$\sin \alpha ,\,\cos \alpha $$ and $$\sqrt {1 + \sin \alpha \cos \alpha } $$ for some $$0 &lt; \alpha  &lt; {\pi  \over 2}$$. Then the greatest angle of the triangle is :

**Solution:**

Let $$a = \sin \alpha ,b = \cos \alpha $$
<br><br>and $$c = \sqrt {1 + \sin \alpha \cos \alpha } $$
<br><br>Clearly $$a$$ and $$b &lt; 1$$ but $$c &gt; 1$$ 
<br><br>as $$\,\,\,\sin \alpha  &gt; 0$$ and $$\cos \alpha  &gt; 0$$
<br><br>$$\therefore$$ $$c$$ is the greatest side and greatest angle is $$C$$
<br><br>$$\therefore$$ $$\cos C = {{{a^2} + {b^2} - {c^2}} \over {2ab}}$$ 
<br><br>$$ = {{{{\sin }^2}\alpha  + {{\cos }^2}\alpha  - 1 - \sin \alpha \cos \alpha } \over {2\,\sin \alpha \cos \alpha }}$$
<br><br>$$ =  - {1 \over 2}$$ 
<br><br>$$\therefore$$ $$C = {120^ \circ }$$
