### Example 1 (Year: 2024)

**Question:**

<p>Let $$e_1$$ be the eccentricity of the hyperbola $$\frac{x^2}{16}-\frac{y^2}{9}=1$$ and $$e_2$$ be the eccentricity of the ellipse $$\frac{x^2}{a^2}+\frac{y^2}{b^2}=1, \mathrm{a} > \mathrm{b}$$, which passes through the foci of the hyperbola. If $$\mathrm{e}_1 \mathrm{e}_2=1$$, then the length of the chord of the ellipse parallel to the $$x$$-axis and passing through $$(0,2)$$ is :</p>

**Solution:**

<p>$$\begin{aligned}
&amp; H: \frac{x^2}{16}-\frac{y^2}{9}=1 \qquad e_1=\frac{5}{4} \\
&amp; \therefore e_1 e_2=1 \Rightarrow e_2=\frac{4}{5}
\end{aligned}$$</p>
<p>Also, ellipse is passing through $$( \pm 5,0)$$</p>
<p>$$\begin{aligned}
&amp; \therefore a=5 \text { and } b=3 \\
&amp; E: \frac{x^2}{25}+\frac{y^2}{9}=1
\end{aligned}$$</p>
