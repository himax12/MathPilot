### Example 1 (Year: 2025)

**Question:**

<p>The number of natural numbers, between 212 and 999, such that the sum of their digits is 15, is _______.</p>

**Solution:**

<p>$$\begin{array}{|c|c|c|}
\hline \mathrm{x} & \mathrm{y} & \mathrm{z} \\
\hline
\end{array}$$</p>
<p>Let $\mathrm{x}=2 \Rightarrow \mathrm{y}+\mathrm{z}=13$</p>
<p>$(4,9),(5,8),(6,7),(7,6),(8,5),(9,4), \rightarrow 6$</p>
<p>Let $x=3 \rightarrow y+z=12$</p>
<p>$(3,9),(4,8), \ldots \ldots . .,(9,3) \rightarrow 7$</p>
<p>Let $x=4 \rightarrow y+z=11$</p>
<p>$(2,9),(3,8), \ldots \ldots \ldots,(9,1) \rightarrow 9$</p>
<p>Let $x=5 \rightarrow y+z=10$</p>
<p>$(1,9),(2,8), \ldots \ldots . .,(9,1) \rightarrow 10$</p>
<p>Let $x=6 \rightarrow y+z=9$</p>
<p>$(0,9),(1,8), \ldots \ldots . .,(9,0) \rightarrow 9$</p>
<p>Let $\mathrm{x}=7 \rightarrow \mathrm{y}+\mathrm{z}=8$</p>
<p>$(0,9),(1,7), \ldots \ldots . .,(8,0) \rightarrow 9$</p>
<p>Let $x=8 \rightarrow y+z=7$</p>
<p>$(0,7),(1,6), \ldots \ldots . .,(7,0) \rightarrow 8$</p>
<p>Let $x=9 \rightarrow y+z=6$</p>
<p>$(0,6),(1,5), \ldots \ldots \ldots,(6,0) \rightarrow 7$</p>
<p>Total $=6=7+8+9+10+9+8+7=64$</p>

### Example 2 (Year: 2025)

**Question:**

<p>If the number of seven-digit numbers, such that the sum of their digits is even, is $m \cdot n \cdot 10^n ; m, n \in\{1,2,3, \ldots, 9\}$, then $m+n$ is equal to__________

**Solution:**

<p>When numbers are uniformly distributed, half of them have even digit sums and half have odd digits sums.</p>
<p>Number of 7-digit numbers with even digit sum =</p>
<p>$$\frac{1}{2} \cdot 9 \cdot 10^6=4.5 \cdot 10^6$$</p>
<p>Note that $9 \cdot 5 \cdot 10^5=4.5 \cdot 10^6$</p>
<p>$$m+n=9+5=14$$</p>
